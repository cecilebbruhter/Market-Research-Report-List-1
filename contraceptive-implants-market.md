<p><p>Contraceptive implants are long-acting reversible contraceptives inserted subdermally to prevent pregnancy. The two primary types are Levonorgestrel and Etonogestrel implants. Levonorgestrel implants, such as Implanon, release the hormone levonorgestrel to inhibit ovulation, effectively preventing pregnancy for up to three years. Etonogestrel implants, such as Nexplanon, use etonogestrel and offer similar efficacy, lasting up to three years as well. Both types provide reliable contraception with minimal intervention, appealing to users seeking long-term birth control options.</p></p>
