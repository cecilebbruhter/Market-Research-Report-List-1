<p><ul><li>Ion-exchange</li><li>High-performance liquid chromatography (HPLC)</li><li>Immunoassay</li><li>Boronate affinity chromatography</li><li>Direct enzymatic assay</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Hemoglobin A1c monitoring market comprises several key types of methodologies. Ion-exchange methods separate hemoglobin variants based on their charge. High-performance liquid chromatography (HPLC) offers precise quantification by using liquid chromatography techniques. Immunoassays utilize antibodies to detect A1c levels, providing high specificity. Boronate affinity chromatography selectively captures glycohemoglobin using boronic acid. Finally, direct enzymatic assays involve enzymatic reactions to measure glucose-bound hemoglobin, allowing for quick and accurate results in diabetes management. Each method has unique advantages for clinical use.</p></p>
